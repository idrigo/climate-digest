Climate Tech VC Hits 3-Year High at $26.1 Billion in H1 2026, Driven by Data Center Power Demand

14 JUL

Currence's H1 2026 Mid-Year Climate Tech Investment Report (formerly Sightline Climate / CTVC) found that venture capital investment in climate technology reached $26.1 billion in the first half of the year — up 55% year-on-year and the strongest H1 since 2022 — with data center-adjacent clean power technologies accounting for 34% of all capital deployed. Two mega-deals in the novel data center infrastructure space, DayOne ($4.5 billion) and Nscale ($2 billion), dominated a record Series C stage that totalled $10.5 billion. Deal count fell 25% to a five-year low as capital concentrated into fewer, later-stage rounds — a structural shift the report attributes to LP caution on early-stage climate bets and growing conviction in proven, infrastructure-linked clean-power business models. Climate management (monitoring, risk, adaptation) had its best H1 since 2022, rising 6%, driven by cheaper satellite capacity and growing corporate climate risk disclosure mandates.

По данным полугодового отчёта Currence, венчурные инвестиции в климатические технологии в H1 2026 достигли $26,1 млрд (+55% г/г, трёхлетний максимум): 34% пришлось на чистую энергетику для дата-центров, включая мегасделки DayOne ($4,5 млрд) и Nscale ($2 млрд). Число сделок упало до пятилетнего минимума — капитал концентрируется на поздних стадиях и инфраструктурных моделях; сегмент климатического управления (мониторинг, риски, адаптация) показал лучшее H1 с 2022 года (+6%).
