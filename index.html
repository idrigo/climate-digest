Climate

The Copernicus Climate Change Service's June 2026 monthly bulletin, officially released July 8–9, confirmed that June 2026 was Western Europe's hottest June on record (regional mean 20.74°C, 3.05°C above the 1991–2020 baseline, topping the 2025 record) and the second-hottest June globally at 16.54°C, with global sea surface temperatures averaging a record 20.86°C — marine heatwave conditions documented across the western Mediterranean and Atlantic coasts. A World Weather Attribution rapid-attribution study published July 7–8 supplied the causal link: the European heatwave would have been "virtually impossible" without fossil-fuel-driven warming, is now 10–100 times more likely than pre-industrial norms, and contributed to more than 2,025 excess deaths in France alone. On policy, Bloomberg's July 8 reporting on the European Commission's forthcoming July 17 ETS review revealed a package trading more free allowances and a slower CO₂ reduction pace for heavy industry against a new €30 billion ETS Investment Booster fund.

Renewables

Germany dominated the renewables news cycle over the three-day window with a landmark H1 2026 result: Fraunhofer ISE analysis published July 8 shows 61.8% of Germany's net public electricity came from renewable sources in the first six months of the year, with solar hitting an all-time H1 record of 43.2 TWh (+10% year-on-year) and offshore wind reaching a record 14.6 TWh (+28%); grid-scale battery storage capacity grew from 25.4 to 29.3 GWh in the same six-month window. The July 7–9 window was otherwise relatively thin for new renewables-specific stories — the major new policy signal, the EU carbon market reform previewed July 8 (see Climate), will carry significant second-order implications for European renewable energy investment through the new €30 billion ETS Investment Booster mechanism.

Proxima Fusion Raises €411 Million in Record European Fusion Round — Google and RWE Invest at €2.4 Billion Valuation; Stellarator Demonstrator to Be Built at Former German Nuclear Site

Munich-based fusion energy startup Proxima Fusion announced on July 7, 2026 that it raised €411 million ($468 million) at a €2.4 billion valuation — the largest European fusion investment on record and the largest single financing round for any fusion company outside of Commonwealth Fusion Systems. The round was led by XTX Ventures and East X Ventures, with Google and German utility RWE as strategic investors alongside KfW Capital, the EU EIC Fund, and a broad syndicate of returning VCs including Balderton, Lightspeed, and DST Global Partners. Proxima's stellarator reactor design avoids the plasma disruption risks of the more widely pursued tokamak; funds will advance the Stellarator Model Coil, expand high-temperature superconducting (HTS) cable and magnet production, and develop manufacturing systems for Alpha, Proxima's net-energy demonstrator, to be built near Munich in partnership with the Max Planck Institute for Plasma Physics and RWE on the site of a former nuclear fission plant in Gundremmingen, Bavaria. RWE's simultaneous roles as investor and site host signals that Europe's major power generators are beginning to treat stellarator fusion as a credible hedge in long-range baseload planning.

Quaise Energy Raises $134 Million in Series B First Close for World's First Superhot Geothermal Power Plant — JERA and Idemitsu Make Strategic Investments

MIT spinout Quaise Energy announced on July 7, 2026 the initial close of a $134 million Series B — bringing total funding to $230 million — to advance Project Obsidian, the world's first commercial superhot geothermal power plant, and continued development of its millimeter-wave drilling system. The round was led by Prelude Ventures with strategic investments from JERA and Idemitsu, two of Japan's largest energy companies. Quaise uses focused millimeter-wave energy originally developed at MIT to ablate rock at depths and temperatures inaccessible to conventional rotary drilling, targeting geothermal heat at 300–500°C in rock formations accessible "in most places worldwide" — an approach that could unlock renewable baseload power beyond the hydrothermal zones where conventional geothermal is viable. The company is approaching one kilometre of depth at its Central Texas field site — which would represent the deepest penetration ever achieved with millimeter-wave drilling — and plans to use the Series B to reach the multi-kilometre depths required for commercial superhot conditions. The strategic backing from JERA and Idemitsu suggests major Asian utilities are hedging post-LNG baseload portfolios against the feasibility risk of grid-scale long-duration storage.

Baltic Storage Platform Completes 200 MW/400 MWh Hertz Battery Hub in Estonia — Hertz 2 Commissioned July 7 on Merchant Terms Without Subsidies

A joint venture of Estonian energy developer Evecon, French independent power producer Corsica Sole, and Paris-based infrastructure fund Mirova has commissioned Hertz 2, a 100 MW/200 MWh battery energy storage system in Aruküla, Harju County, Estonia, formally opened July 7, 2026 — completing the two-project Hertz battery hub alongside its sister facility Hertz 1 at a combined 200 MW and 400 MWh capacity. Both systems are connected to the grid of Estonian transmission system operator Elering and will provide fast-response frequency regulation services alongside day-ahead and intraday electricity market participation, addressing a strategically critical gap as Estonia, Latvia, and Lithuania finalise desynchronisation from the Russian/Belarusian power system and full integration with the continental European grid. The €170 million investment was made entirely with private capital and without direct subsidies, financed through an €85.6 million loan facility provided jointly by the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development, the Nordic Investment Bank, and Edmond de Rothschild Asset Management. Evecon CEO Karl-Joonatan Kvell described the subsidy-free investment decision as a signal that grid-scale battery storage is commercially viable on merchant terms in Baltic electricity markets.
