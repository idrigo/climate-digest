Climate

The window was dominated by the human and scientific reckoning with Europe's record-shattering early-summer heatwave: the WHO tied 1,300+ excess deaths to the heat, and a rapid attribution study found the event would have been "virtually impossible" without human-caused warming. On policy, the World Bank formally retired its quantitative climate-finance lending targets under US pressure — a notable retreat in multilateral climate finance — while scientists openly debated whether the relentless heat marks a structurally new climate for the fastest-warming continent.

Weather over the window was overwhelmingly about Europe's record heatwave, which peaked in the west around 27 June and is now shifting east toward the Balkans and Eastern Europe, breaking national records and fuelling wildfires. The Atlantic remained quiet with no active tropical cyclones, while the Eastern Pacific began spinning up its next system off Baja California, and the US continued an unusually active severe-weather stretch across the Plains and Upper Midwest.

Renewables

The window crystallised the US clean-energy retreat under the Trump administration: a fourth federal offshore-wind buyout (Duke Energy's 1.6 GW Carolina Long Bay) and Air Products' scrapping of its flagship Louisiana clean-hydrogen complex (a ~$2.9B charge). Against that retreat, solar momentum continued — highlighted by a first-of-its-kind "surplus interconnection" strategy that reused a gas plant's grid tie to fast-track 270 MW in Illinois — even as First Solar faced investor litigation over tariff disclosures and South Korea advanced an 800 MW offshore wind tender.

Industry News

A quiet window for climate and renewable-energy dealmaking, dominated by offtake and consortium activity rather than headline M&A or large venture rounds. The clearest in-window items were a first-of-its-kind utility offshore-wind PPA from Uniper and a major industrial-finance consortium winning South Korea's 800 MW Jeonbuk tender — both signs of demand and capital re-engaging with offshore wind even as the US sector retreats.
