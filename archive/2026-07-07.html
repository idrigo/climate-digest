Renewables

Eurostat confirmed on July 1 that renewable energy sources supplied 45.5% of EU electricity in Q1 2026 — the highest first-quarter share ever recorded in the 27-member bloc, building on the full-year 2025 milestone in which solar and wind generated more EU electricity than fossil fuels for the first time in history. The figure is particularly notable because Q1 typically represents the lowest-solar period of the European year, underscoring the degree to which expanded wind capacity and improved hydro conditions drove the first-quarter result. In the US, the first days after the OBBBA's July 4 wind and solar construction deadline confirm the market shift visible since the law's passage: battery storage, geothermal, and nuclear are absorbing investment capital at an accelerating pace, while developers with grandfathered wind and solar projects rush to reach commercial operation ahead of the December 31, 2027 placed-in-service cutoff.

Industry News

The week's defining deal is KKR's $4.2 billion acquisition of EDF's North American renewables and storage operations — the largest single renewable energy asset sale in North America this year and KKR's biggest individual bet on the energy transition to date, giving the private equity firm direct control over 5.6 GW of operating clean energy capacity. The transaction also reflects EDF's strategic retrenchment to fund its domestic nuclear revival programme, illustrating how European utilities are treating North American renewables portfolios as liquid capital rather than long-term strategic assets — a dynamic that may accelerate further as OBBBA policy uncertainty reduces the appeal of holding non-grandfathered US wind and solar development pipelines.

KKR Acquires EDF Power Solutions' North American Renewables Portfolio for $4.2 Billion — Becomes Top-Ten US Clean Energy Owner

Global private equity firm KKR has agreed to acquire the entire US and Canadian operations of EDF Power Solutions — the North American renewables and storage subsidiary of French state-owned utility EDF — for approximately $4.2 billion in equity value, plus up to $390 million in additional payments contingent on portfolio performance. The acquired portfolio spans 5.6 GW of net installed capacity across solar, onshore wind, and battery storage facilities in the US and Canada, making EDF Power Solutions one of the top-ten renewable energy owners in the United States by installed capacity. The deal is KKR's largest single investment in the renewables sector and reflects its thesis that grid-scale clean energy assets — particularly operating, cash-flowing projects with long-term power purchase agreements — are attractive infrastructure holdings as electricity demand grows from data-centre and industrial electrification. For EDF, the divestiture accelerates capital generation for its domestic nuclear programme in France, where the utility is committed to constructing 14 new EPR2 reactors through 2050 under the government's nuclear revival plan.
