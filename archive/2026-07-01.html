Industry News

Equinor Completes its Offshore Wind Retreat — Japan Exit Caps Multi-Year Pullback from Pure Wind

Equinor confirmed on June 26 that it will close its Tokyo office by the end of 2026 and exit Japan's offshore wind market, ending an eight-year effort that secured zero leases from successive government tenders. The announcement came just ten days after the Oslo-based company scrapped its 2030 installed renewables capacity target entirely — previously set at 50 GW — and pivoted its strategy to "integrated power," combining natural gas generation with renewables rather than pursuing standalone offshore wind. The Japan exit follows earlier retreats from Vietnam, Spain, Portugal, and France, leaving Equinor's residual offshore wind portfolio effectively confined to legacy positions in the North Sea and off the US East Coast. The strategic shift underscores the mounting pressure on major integrated energy companies to justify pure offshore wind investments against rising capital costs and shifting policy landscapes.

Seven States Sue Over Trump's Offshore Wind Buyouts; Congressional Probe Opens as Total Nears $2.6 Billion

The attorneys general of seven northeastern states — New York, New Jersey, Connecticut, Maine, Massachusetts, Rhode Island, and Vermont — filed a federal lawsuit challenging the Trump administration's practice of paying Interior Department-brokered deals for developers to surrender federal offshore wind leases. The plaintiffs argue Congress never authorised the use of the US Treasury's Judgment Fund for such transactions, and that the deals systematically redirect public funds toward fossil fuel projects. The suit joins California's separate pending challenge and a new Congressional oversight investigation into whether the eight buyout deals — now totalling approximately $2.6 billion since March 2025 — constitute a misuse of public funds. The combination of multistate litigation and Congressional scrutiny marks a significant legal escalation that could constrain or halt further buyout agreements.

Engineering News-Record

ECMWF Achieves Global NWP Milestone: First-Ever Operational Assimilation of Spaceborne Cloud Radar

The European Centre for Medium-Range Weather Forecasts announced that since June 10 it has been operationally assimilating cloud-profiling radar data from the ESA-JAXA EarthCARE satellite into its Integrated Forecasting System — the first time spaceborne cloud radar has been incorporated into any operational global numerical weather prediction model. EarthCARE's Cloud Profiling Radar delivers vertical cross-sections of cloud and precipitation structure with global coverage and a 60-minute revisit time, directly improving how the IFS represents clouds, convective systems, and rainfall. For weather-sensitive industries — renewable energy dispatch, aviation, reinsurance, and agriculture — more accurate cloud and precipitation forecasts translate directly into operational and financial value, and the milestone marks a new generation of observation data beginning to meaningfully improve real-world forecast accuracy.
