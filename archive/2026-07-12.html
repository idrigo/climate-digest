Three distinct developments updated the climate science and policy picture in the July 9–12 window. The Trump White House appointed geochemist and climate critic Matthew Wielicki to lead the US National Climate Assessment program — the statutory review coordinated across 13 federal agencies including NOAA — raising immediate questions about the scientific integrity of the country's most authoritative domestic climate report.
