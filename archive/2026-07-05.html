Climate

The Copernicus Climate Change Service published its June 2026 monthly climate bulletin, confirming last month as the hottest June in the instrumental record — global surface air temperatures ran 0.5°C above the 1991–2020 reference period, substantially exceeding June 2019's previous record. ECMWF simultaneously reported a landmark for operational numerical weather prediction: data from EarthCARE's cloud profiling radar are now being routinely assimilated into the global forecast system, the first time any forecasting centre has ingested spaceborne cloud radar observations operationally. A World Weather Attribution analysis framing the ongoing European heatwave sequence underscores that such events have been made dramatically more likely — and more intense — by fossil fuel emissions over recent decades.

  • Copernicus C3S Copernicus confirms June 2026 as hottest June on global record — surface air temperatures 0.5°C above 1991–2020 average; record North Atlantic warmth; El Niño amplification on top of long-term warming trend driving the new benchmark The C3S June 2026 monthly climate bulletin reports that global average surface air temperature for June 2026 was just over 0.5°C above the 1991–2020 reference average, making it the warmest June since records began — exceeding June 2019, the previous record holder, by a substantial margin; the North Atlantic simultaneously set its own June sea-surface temperature record, sustaining the anomalous ocean warmth that has persisted since late 2023; the bulletin notes that with El Niño now established and strengthening, 2026 is on track for new annual mean temperature records in both the ocean and atmosphere; C3S director Carlo Buontempo described the sequence of record-breaking months as reflecting an accelerating shift in the background state of the climate system.
  • ECMWF World first: ECMWF assimilates EarthCARE cloud profiling radar data into operational global forecast system from June 10, 2026 — first spaceborne cloud radar ever ingested operationally by any NWP centre Published July 1, 2026: ECMWF's Integrated Forecasting System (IFS) has been ingesting cloud reflectivity profiles from the ESA–JAXA EarthCARE satellite's Cloud Profiling Radar since June 10, 2026 — the first operational assimilation of spaceborne cloud radar observations in NWP history; the data provide direct vertical profiles of cloud structure and precipitation intensity that were previously unavailable to models, constraining ice/liquid partitioning, particle size, and fall velocity within clouds; early verification scores show improved cloud representation, particularly in dynamically active mid-latitude systems where cloud feedbacks are known to amplify forecast errors; the achievement is expected to improve short- to medium-range rainfall and severe weather forecasts globally.
  • World Weather Attribution Fossil fuel emissions have rapidly worsened European heatwaves in just a few decades, WWA finds — 2026 June–July heatwave sequence among the most extreme in European history; such events virtually impossible without human-caused warming The World Weather Attribution collaboration published a rapid attribution analysis concluding that the 2026 European heatwave sequence is representative of the type of event that has been made substantially more likely and more intense by anthropogenic greenhouse gas emissions; temperatures recorded during the June 2026 heatwave events — including 44.3°C in Pissos, France and 43.7°C in Tama, Spain — were 5–12°C above seasonal averages and consistent with late-21st-century climate projections under current emission trajectories; the analysis finds that such events, which might have been expected once in several decades in the pre-industrial climate, now occur with a frequency and intensity consistent with 1.5°C of global warming — and will worsen further if warming continues above current levels.

In the UK, tomorrow's publication of AR8 Contracts for Difference statutory notices marks the official start of the next annual offshore wind auction cycle, with a record-setting AR7 baseline behind it. Massachusetts's Senate passed a sweeping $14 billion energy affordability bill this week that recalibrates the state's offshore wind procurement pace in light of federal policy changes.

BeZero Carbon Acquires Cedar to Bring AI-Driven Carbon Accounting Into Its Ratings Platform

London-based BeZero Carbon, the world's largest provider of independent carbon credit ratings by contracted volume, acquired Cedar, a New York-based startup whose software-agent platform automates carbon accounting, renewable energy analysis, and ESG due diligence for institutional investors and corporates. Financial terms were not disclosed, but all three Cedar co-founders join BeZero's team. The deal deepens BeZero's ability to ingest sensor data, satellite imagery, supply-chain records, and registry information into a single automated verification workflow — directly targeting the credibility gap that collapsed voluntary carbon markets in 2023–24, when certifiers were compensated by the developers whose claims they assessed. The acquisition comes as institutional demand for rigorous third-party carbon ratings is rising sharply ahead of EU ESG disclosure regulation and the growing volume of Article 6 bilateral carbon trading under the Paris Agreement.

Gaussion Raises £21 Million to Commercialise Magnetic-Field Battery Intelligence for EVs and Grid Storage

London-based Gaussion, a University College London spinout, closed a £21 million ($28 million) round led by AlbionVC and BGF — joined by US deep-tech investor Future Ventures, mobility specialist Autotech Ventures, and the UCL Technology Fund — to commercialise its AI-enabled control software that applies an externally generated magnetic field to lithium battery cells during charge and discharge. The magnetic field steers ion flow to enable faster charging and significantly reduce cell degradation, with no modification to battery chemistry or pack design, making the technology deployable across existing EV charging infrastructure, grid-scale battery energy storage systems, and drone platforms. The round pushes total BGF investment in UK and Irish businesses past £5 billion and reflects growing investor appetite for battery performance optimisation as BESS costs hit record lows and renewable-plus-storage project pipelines accelerate globally — with grid-scale storage now positioned as the primary beneficiary of clean energy capital flows in the post-OBBBA US market.

MCi Carbon Opens World's First Industrial Carbon Refinery in Newcastle — Now Seeking $50 Million to Scale

Australian clean technology company MCi Carbon has opened Myrtle — described as the world's first fully integrated, multi-purpose industrial carbon refinery — on Kooragang Island in Newcastle, Australia, converting captured industrial CO₂ into commercially valuable construction minerals including magnesium carbonates, silica, and precipitated calcium carbonate. The company has since launched a search for up to US$50 million in new investment to expand production capacity and advance commercial-scale deployment, following A$14.5 million in prior Australian government Carbon Capture Technologies Programme grants. Unlike geological storage or ocean injection approaches, MCi's process uses CO₂ as a manufacturing feedstock with defined revenue-generating output products — a model that seven of the top 20 funded carbon capture startups now share, reflecting investor preference for CCUS approaches that generate cash flow rather than requiring ongoing cost-of-abatement financing. The fundraise comes as Article 6 markets and enterprise demand for supply-chain-verified carbon removal solutions expand ahead of mandatory EU and Australian corporate disclosure frameworks.
